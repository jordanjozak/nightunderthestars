I · The Invitation

An evening that asks something else of you.

Night Under the Stars is a small, intentional gathering. A handful of people, brought to a quiet place to spend a weekend together — not at an event, not at a conference, but in the kind of stretched, contemplative time that has become rare.

There is no agenda we are trying to convince you of, no apparatus you need to be wired to. The weekend opens an unhurried space for stillness, for conversation, and for the kinds of experiences that have always been available to those who slow down enough to notice.

We do not promise the phenomenon will arrive. We are simply creating the conditions that have, across many traditions and many lives, made it possible.

If you found your way here after hearing the name somewhere — welcome. This page is the door. The list below is how you hear when the next night opens.

III · Your Host

Time with someone doing the work.

Jordan Jozak
Disclosure Advocate · Host

Jordan has worked publicly as an entrepreneur for over a decade, with successful scale-ups in technology and utilities. In April 2024 he came forward as “Martin,” a pseudonymous voice on Substack writing about classified consciousness research, before revealing his identity later that year. He joined Skywatcher as a psionic asset alongside several operators who have since stepped into the public arena, and has been featured in investigative journalist Ross Coulthart’s reporting on the field.

His current work brings firsthand experience of exotic technology and non-human intelligence into the open, building bridges from the disclosure domain to ordinary life. Night Under the Stars is the most personal of those bridges: a weekend at a time, a sky at a time.

Voices from the Network

Each gathering draws practitioners and guests from the broader field — experiencers, researchers, contemplatives — chosen for the night and the circle. Their presence shapes the conversation, and the conversation shapes the weekend.

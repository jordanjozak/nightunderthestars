Night Under The Stars

A guided meditation experience beneath the cosmos

Where consciousness meets the infinite. Where we remember what we've always known.

The Experience

Night Under The Stars is an invitation to turn inward together. Through guided meditation with a shared audio experience, we create space for individual journeys within collective presence.

Each gathering begins with a 30-minute meditation featuring an exclusive playlist shared through personal headphones. We synchronize our experience while honoring each person's unique path. After the meditation, we share the silence together beneath the stars, often witnessing phenomena that emerge through our collective attention.

What to Bring

  • Cozy, warm clothes (we'll be outside under the stars)
  • Audio headphones or earphones
  • Any meditative tools that help you access your "space" (eye masks, crystals, journals)
  • An open and curious wonder toward the world we share

"This is about remembering. About returning to what's always been here, waiting beneath the noise."

The Esalen Meditation

You don't need to wait for a gathering. The Esalen meditation is available for you to practice solo, anywhere under the stars.

This 30-minute journey guides you through heart-centered awareness, inviting you to visualize green light radiating from your heart center—the frequency of love, connection, and healing. As you rest under the night sky, you become both witness and participant in the vast conversation between consciousness and cosmos.

The Practice

Find a comfortable place outside where you can see the stars. Bring headphones, something warm to wear, and perhaps a blanket or mat. Press play on the playlist below and allow yourself to be guided.

The meditation invites you to breathe green light through your heart chakra—not forcing, just allowing. This is the color of nature, of growth, of unconditional love. As you breathe, imagine this light expanding from your chest, filling your body, then radiating outward to touch the earth, the sky, the stars themselves.

You may notice unusual phenomena. You may feel profound peace. You may experience both. Whatever arises, trust it. You're remembering an ancient practice of conscious communion with the universe.

Tips for Solo Practice

  • Choose a night with clear skies when possible
  • Allow 45-60 minutes total (meditation plus integration time)
  • Keep a journal nearby to capture any insights afterward
  • Trust whatever arises—there's no "right" experience
  • Share your experience with us if you feel called
